Transforming Parent Engagement on the Connexus Student Dashboard.

To strengthen collaboration and engagement across the Connexus EdTech learning platform, Pearson needed a unified, intuitive way for parents, teachers, and students to access essential academic information. Analytics, direct correspondence, and numerous support tickets exposed a fragmented, inefficient dashboard that hindered meaningful interaction. We redesigned the experience to consolidate key updates, performance insights, and communication tools into one seamless, user-friendly hub—empowering families and educators to stay connected, informed, and actively involved in student success.

Business Goal

Pearson needed to strengthen engagement across its Connexus EdTech learning platform. Analytics, parent feedback, and support tickets exposed a fragmented student dashboard that frustrated parents, teachers, and students. A unified, intuitive hub was essential to improve transparency, simplify communication, and keep families actively involved in learning.

User Benefit

For parents and students, the goal was to provide clear, actionable insights—making it effortless to track progress, view tasks, communicate with teachers, and stay informed without navigating multiple confusing tools. The experience empowered families, reduced frustration, and built trust in the platform.

Process

I focused on creating a seamless, transparent experience that consolidated courses, scores, alerts, and teacher contact details into one accessible dashboard. The redesign aimed to bridge communication gaps, reduce support requests, and encourage meaningful parent participation in student success.

01. Product Definition

We were tasked with redesigning the Connexus Student Dashboard as a single, intuitive hub for courses, assignments, progress tracking, and teacher communication—streamlining information access for parents, students, and educators.

02. Research

We conducted stakeholder interviews, surveys with 50 parents, and in-depth interviews with 15 parents. Findings confirmed pain points: scattered data, limited visibility into progress, and complex communication channels. A competitive analysis of EdTech platforms like Canvas and PowerSchool highlighted opportunities to differentiate with clarity and transparency.

03. Analysis

Insights revealed engagement suffered due to fragmented resources and unclear data presentation. Parents needed timely alerts, easy-to-read scores, and direct access to teachers. These gaps shaped our requirements for a simplified, engaging dashboard.

04. Design

We iterated from sketches to wireframes to high-fidelity designs. Prototypes featured a clean layout, visual progress indicators, and easy email initiation with teachers. Using Pearson’s design system ensured scalability and brand consistency.

05. Testing

Internal and user testing with 10 parents validated navigation and task flows. Feedback led to clearer labels, streamlined notifications, and improved teacher visibility. Iterations refined the experience for quick, confident interactions.

06. Implementation

Collaborating with UX, Product, Marketing, and offshore engineering, we delivered a unified, user-centered dashboard. Post-launch monitoring showed fewer support tickets, stronger parent engagement, and improved trust—demonstrating the value of thoughtful UX in EdTech.
