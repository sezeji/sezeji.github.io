David Elesinmogun, Esq.

David is a founding partner of Elesinmogun & Egwuatu. An attorney licensed in both the U.S and Nigeria with over 30 years of legal experience. He specializes in advising companies that are investing in the Nigerian and African market. David advises on compliance with U.S., Nigerian and international anti-corruption measures such as the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act. David works with clients by developing anti-corruption compliance programs, performing due diligence , providing training and investigations on risky business practices.

David worked as a prosecutor in the criminal litigation division of the Lagos State Ministry of Justice, litigating cases involving environmental law violations. David has extensive experience in representing business entities. He has represented clients in the areas of banking, energy, oil and gas, and telecommunications. He has also used his expertise to counsel clients on forming joint ventures. Today, he specializes in advising his diverse business clients about the legal and regulatory standards of transacting business abroad and facilitates his clients' entry into international markets.

Obumneme Egwuatu, Esq.

Obumneme is a founding partner of Elesinmogun & Egwuatu. Mr. Egwuatu advises on compliance with U.S. and international anti-corruption measures such as the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act as well as U.S. anti-money laundering laws and export controls. Obumneme works with clients by developing anti-corruption compliance programs, performing due diligence , providing training and investigations on risky business practices . He specializes in companies that are investing in the Nigerian and African market. In addition to his strong background in international law, he also has a solid background in corporate law, international arbitration, international trade law and transactional practice.

After graduating from Middlesex University Law School in London, England in 1999, he received an LL.M. in International and Comparative Law from George Washington University Law School. Obumneme subsequently became a member of the New York Bar in 2003, a member of the Maryland Bar in 2007 and the District of Columbia Bar in 2010. He also worked as an intern at The Africa Fund, where he gained valuable experience analyzing and researching African issues related to domestic/international policies, international trade, protection of human rights and the advancement of democracy.

Obumneme is the current Vice-President of an NGO in Nigeria (Health and Economic Empowerment for Women). Health and Economic Empowerment for Women empowers women to improve their health and economic well-being so as to contribute towards the improvement of the overall Human Development Index of the country.

Julius A. Ugese, Esq.

Julius Ugese is a senior associate at Elesinmogun & Egwuatu. Mr. Ugese is a law graduate of Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria and was called to the Nigerian Bar as a Barrister in 2003. His areas of practice are Real Estate, insolvency/receivership, debt recovery, corporate law and contract negotiation.

Julius has worked on a wide range of domestic and international law disputes. He has represented and advised clients in complex transactions involving multiple jurisdictions and laws. Julius is an experienced litigator and has appeared in the high court and appellate courts in Nigeria. He is also actively involved in the firm's arbitration and dispute resolution practice.

Mr. Ugese is a member of the Nigerian Bar Association and a Solicitor and Advocate of the Supreme Court of Nigeria.

Chinazo Egwuatu

Chinazo is a consultant solicitor at Elesinmogun & Egwuatu. She obtained her Law degree at University of London and subsequently attended London Metropolitan University where she completed her Legal Practice Course (LPC) with a Commendation. Chinazo completed her two year training contract and was admitted to the roll of Solicitors of England and Wales in 2011 and has gone on to specialise in different areas of law including Commercial law.

Chinazo maintains close ties with her home country Nigeria and is a member of British Nigeria Law Forum (BNLF). Chinazo is also currently one of the trustees on the board of Health and Economic Empowerment for Women (HEEW), a Non-Governmental Organisation whose aim to empower women to health and economic wellbeing in order to become financially independent.
