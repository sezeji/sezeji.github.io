Sherwin-Williams

Boosting painters’ efficiency with precise, easy-to-follow spectro calibration.

Sherwin-Williams understands that even small color-matching errors can waste valuable time and resources in automotive refinishing. To address this, we created a simple, intuitive spectrophotometer calibration wizard that removes complexity, improves accuracy, and speeds up the painting process. I designed an approachable, efficient solution that empowers painters of all experience levels to work faster, waste less, and feel confident in every job.

Business Goal

In an automotive paint shop, every minute in the booth matters. Keeping the spectrophotometer properly calibrated ensures that each color scan is accurate the first time. This boosts job output by producing more first-time-right finishes per booth hour—fewer tint loops, fewer re-sprays, and less wasted paint.

Reliable calibration maximizes resources, reduces costly rework, and keeps schedules on track, delivering smoother operations and stronger margins.

User Benefit

For painters, the goal was to “give instant confidence in color scans” by making spectrophotometer readiness clear at a glance, calibration quick and intuitive, and error recovery simple.

This approach helps painters stay on schedule, avoid costly re-sprays, and focus on delivering flawless, first-time-right finishes—without feeling slowed down or stressed by complex tools or processes.

Process

I focused on creating an intuitive, reliable experience that made spectrophotometer calibration effortless for automotive painters—helping them check readiness at a glance, recover from errors with ease, and achieve first-time-right color matches.

01. Product Definition

We were tasked with creating a design that allows automotive shops to easily track and manage calibration for spectrophotometers, helping them maintain accurate, first-time-right color matches and avoid costly rework or delays by providing a clear, at-a-glance status view and a streamlined, calibration workflow.

02. Research

We studied painters and managers frustrated by unclear calibration tracking. They signaled a strong need for a simple, at-a-glance view and an effortless way to start calibration. With this insight, I explored streamlined layouts and flows to guide them smoothly through checking status and completing calibration with confidence.

03. Analysis

Painters often missed calibration cues or found existing systems confusing, causing delays and rework. Managers needed a reliable, centralized view to avoid bottlenecks and protect productivity. We distilled this into key needs: clear status at a glance, intuitive indicators, and a single guided path to start calibration—reducing errors and keeping operations smooth.

04. Design

Using these insights, we crafted a clean, high-contrast interface with clear color-coded status cards for each device. Prominent calibration due dates and a single action button provide instant clarity and reduce cognitive load. The design emphasizes readability under booth lighting and guides users effortlessly through calibration to ensure accuracy and productivity.

05. Testing

For testing, we conducted usability sessions with painters and managers using interactive prototypes in realistic booth conditions. We evaluated clarity of status indicators, ease of finding due calibrations, and the efficiency of the one-click wizard. Feedback guided refinements to ensure fast comprehension, smooth interactions, and minimal disruption to workflow.

06. Implementation

During implementation, we collaborated with developers and shop managers to deploy the landing page into live paint shop environments. We monitored calibration compliance, error rates, and booth throughput, providing onboarding and support for painters. Continuous feedback informed minor adjustments, ensuring a smooth rollout, improved efficiency, and measurable business impact.
